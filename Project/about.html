About Me

My name is Ethan Emmanuel Isakir, and I am a 10th-grade student at The Browning School in New York City. Growing up in Forest Hills, Queens has given me a strong appreciation for both academics and teamwork. I am passionate about business and aspire to pursue a career in entrepreneurship or finance, where I can combine strategic thinking with real-world problem solving.

I am actively involved in athletics, participating in Track, Soccer, and Ping Pong. These sports have taught me the importance of discipline, perseverance, and collaboration. In addition, I volunteer at MoMath (the National Museum of Mathematics), where I help engage visitors with interactive math exhibits. This experience has strengthened my communication skills and shown me the value of making complex ideas accessible.

My academic interests include history and global studies, highlighted by my enrollment in AP World History. I am determined to continue building a strong foundation in business, leadership, and analytical thinking as I prepare for college and my future career.
